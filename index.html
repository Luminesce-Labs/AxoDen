The governing thesis

"The properties that determine whether AI reasoning is certifiable are topological invariants of the evidence graph — not statistics of model outputs."

Yalcinkaya, E. (2026). ARIA Mathematics for Safe AI application. Luminesce Limited.

Aviation software engineering resolved the testing problem fifty years ago. DO-178C requires structural proof of the design rather than exhaustive testing of outputs. AI safety has not made this transition.

AxoDen makes it. The framework does not prove individual models correct — that remains intractable at production scale. It proves the correctness of the governance architecture around the model. The kernel's guarantees are independent of which model is used inside it.

The result: entropy budgets, independence scores, topology certificates, and cryptographic ledger records — artefacts that existing safety assessors already know how to evaluate. No new certification path required.

The structural gap

Why regulated industries cannot certify AI today

Regulators under the EU AI Act, ISO 26262, DO-178C, IEC 61508, and IEC 62304 require formal certification evidence for high-stakes AI. ISO/IEC TR 5469:2024 identifies the gap explicitly: no existing approach produces compositional independence certificates from structural proof rather than statistical testing.

Gap 01

Behavioural testing cannot provide structural guarantees

No finite test suite covers the input space of a real system. Vendors produce statistical dashboards. Assessors need structural certificates. The gap is categorical, not quantitative.

Gap 02

Independence is assumed, never engineered or proved

Every multi-agent safety architecture implicitly assumes constituent nodes fail independently. When correlated failures occur, Byzantine assumptions break and audit chains are silently invalidated. No published framework produces a constructive independence certificate of this form — the gap is identified in the ARIA Mathematics for Safe AI programme call.

Gap 03

RF and sensor modalities have no certification path at all

The NASEM workshop on non-linguistic AI (August 2025) named four specific RF-AI integration barriers with no current solution: no natural token boundaries, throughput mismatch, Shannon-limit ambiguity, and common-mode failure risk.

The AxoDen answer

Every existing approach treats independence as something to be observed. AxoDen treats it as something to be engineered and proved. This is the distinction ARIA identifies as missing from the field.

EBDP

Entropy-Bounded Data Pipelines

Hard information-theoretic ceilings H(Xk | Xk-1) ≤ Hmaxk on uncertainty accumulation at every pipeline stage. Uncertainty cannot silently compound.

EFI

Entropy-Frugal Intelligence

Stage-level influence bounds I(Xk; Mk) ≤ εk detect and reject fabricated outputs by bounding the information a model may introduce without evidential backing. Hallucinations are structurally bounded.

CFS

Coprime-Factor Security

Independence engineered across four orthogonal planes: cryptographic, vendor, supply-chain, and operational. CMRI := sup ρ̄ij(ω) ≤ 1 − ISmin + εA. A design constraint, not a dashboard reading.

AxoDen was built from a concrete observation: safety-critical AI deployments consistently fail at the governance layer, not the model layer. Red-teaming, constitutional AI, and runtime monitoring all detect failure. AxoDen is designed to prevent it.

The work was built entirely in evenings and weekends alongside a full-time consulting contract until January 2026, without institutional support or external funding. The result is a coherent, versioned body of work with a stable platform nucleus and two active wedge lines — not a proposal for work that might be done.

The theoretical foundations already exist. They are DOI-registered, version-controlled, and linked to active downstream runtime work, with current implementation posture tracked in the March 2026 canon. Active submissions to ARIA Mathematics for Safe AI, ENCODE Fellowship, LTFF, and related programmes are intended to fund machine-checking, independent validation, and standards-body engagement.

The domain breadth remains important, but it now serves a clearer portfolio structure. Cybersecurity and RF-AI are the two active wedge paths. Microgrids, industrial control, medical devices, autonomous vehicles, and related cyber-physical systems remain evidence that the kernel generalises beyond a single market.
