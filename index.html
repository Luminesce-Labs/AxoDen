Design principles

Three contracts. Every product.

01

Kernel-first

A shared control vocabulary, evidence identity rules, and determinism contracts are fixed at the platform layer. Domain products import these rather than re-implementing them, so semantics and audit posture cannot drift across deployments.

02

Fail-closed by contract

When the declared operating envelope is violated, the system degrades, inhibits, or halts — and records why. Safe degradation is not a fallback. It is a first-class output with a logged, auditable decision record.

03

Replay over rhetoric

Every decision is designed to be independently re-run and assessed against the original evidence. Replayability is the assurance claim, not a confidence score. Third parties can verify findings without accessing the live environment.

Previously undocumented · February 2026

A structural safety hazard at the
digital-physical
boundary.

01 · The discovery

Many safety-critical systems implement write-ahead logging: record intent, then execute. In pure data systems, replaying a logged intent is safe — the target is a database record. At the digital-physical boundary, it is not. An intent recorded at time t₀ may be replayed after an outage or restart, executing against physical conditions that have changed by time t₁. The system believes it is following correct discipline. The actuation is unsafe. This structural hazard — arising from the architecture itself, not from adversarial action — had not been previously documented in the safety or systems engineering literature.

02 · Formal characterisation and solution

We formally characterised this as the Zombie Intent Hazard and documented it in a DOI-registered paper (10.5281/zenodo.18768569, February 2026). The TLA+-verified solution is session-bound intent validation at the moment of execution: an intent is only admissible if its originating session is still valid and the physical preconditions recorded at intent-formation time still hold. During independent architectural review, GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini each failed to identify the hazard — corroborating that it is non-obvious and not surfaced by behavioural review.

03 · Implementation

Session-bound intent validation is now a first-class safety invariant in the AxoDen kernel. The Grid Flight Recorder — currently in design — is the domain product that enforces this contract in cyber-physical deployments: no irreversible physical actuation occurs unless safety, policy, and evidence conditions are valid at the moment of execution, not merely at the moment of intent formation.

About

Luminesce Limited is a UK deep-tech company founded by Erkan Yalcinkaya, Engineering Physicist, with 25 years of experience delivering technology in regulated, safety-critical environments.

That background shaped a single design requirement: assurance evidence must be the kind that assessors, regulators, and governance boards already know how to evaluate — replayable, auditable, and contract-bound. Not a new certification path. Artefacts that fit the paths that already exist.

Luminesce combines mathematical rigour — first-principles constraints and evidence formation — with deployment reality: what regulated operations require in practice. AxoDen is the result: a kernel-and-wedges product line for deterministic assurance middleware in safety-critical AI.

Open to mission partnerships (government and defence), academic research partnerships, and industrial pilots in security operations, RF-AI integration, and regulated infrastructure.
