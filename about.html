Biography

From regulated systems to structural AI assurance

The AxoDen thesis comes from direct experience inside environments where certification evidence is scrutinised by assessors, not imagined by product teams.

Erkan Yalcinkaya is an engineering physicist, systems architect, and the sole author of the AxoDen framework. With 25 years of experience across regulated industries, complex systems, software architecture, and applied AI, his work pioneers the emerging discipline of Information Physics, shifting AI safety from behavioural heuristics to certifiable mathematical structure.

Having spent two decades inside highly regulated enterprises, Erkan understands exactly what certification evidence looks like from an assessor's side of the table. AxoDen was built from a harsh, practical observation: safety-critical AI consistently fails at the governance layer because the industry is trapped managing the bureaucracy of risk rather than the physics of risk. By converting static assumptions into a dynamic Reflexive Epistemic Fabric, his framework ensures that AI systems are mathematically bounded by their structural geometry.

AxoDen is the result of three years of solo development alongside full-time consulting. Before a single grant application was submitted, Erkan delivered 17 DOI-registered publications and four fully implemented, tested systems. This is not academic theory; the work is complete, not proposed.

Operating thesis

Certification fails when AI governance is treated as paperwork instead of physics.

AxoDen reframes safety as a structural property of the evidence graph and governance fabric. The point is not to add another monitoring dashboard. The point is to make safety claims certifiable in the same way other engineering disciplines certify bounded systems.
