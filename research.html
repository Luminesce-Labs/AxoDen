The Zombie Intent Hazard: A Structural Safety Vulnerability in Write-Ahead AI Architectures

Formally characterises and names the Zombie Intent Hazard: a structural failure class in any system where AI decisions are queued, buffered, or persisted before physical execution. State desynchronisation between intent computation and execution context causes irreversible physical actions to occur under conditions for which the decision was never valid. TLA+ specification covers 52 named state transitions across the complete intent lifecycle. Verified across 2.4 million reachable states with zero counterexamples for three core safety invariants. Independently submitted to GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini during architectural review: all three systems failed to identify the structural failure class. Countermeasures: EOA-Latch, reboot-safe session binding, One-Time Actuation Tickets.
