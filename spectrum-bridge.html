The four NASEM barriers — August 2025

Four RF-AI integration problems with no current solution

The RF-to-AI boundary has a distinct assurance problem. Spectrum Bridge is designed around four practical issues identified in the August 2025 NASEM workshop.

NASEM Barrier 1

No natural token boundaries

Continuous RF waveforms do not break neatly into tokens in the way text does.

Spectrum Bridge derives its handling rules from the waveform itself rather than forcing an arbitrary token scheme.

NASEM Barrier 2

Throughput mismatch

RF data moves much faster than downstream AI systems can process it directly.

Spectrum Bridge controls downsampling and quantisation so evidence quality does not become an afterthought.

NASEM Barrier 3

Shannon-limit ambiguity

Some signals are physically ambiguous even when an AI system sounds confident about them.

Spectrum Bridge blocks impossible or ambiguous inputs before they reach inference.

NASEM Barrier 4

Common-mode failure risk

Redundant systems can still fail together when they depend on the same physical signal path.

Spectrum Bridge treats shared infrastructure as part of the safety problem, not something to ignore.

Execution path

From RF signal to admissible evidence

The workflow is designed to keep weak or ambiguous signal evidence from quietly turning into AI decisions.

Step 01

Active development

Scale and quantise the signal

The system prepares the signal in a deterministic way so the evidence chain starts from a stable, reviewable input.

Step 02

Active development

Apply physics and admissibility gates

Step 03

Parallel design

Emit reviewable evidence

Operating envelope

Four-state MQ operating envelope enforced at the RF boundary

Every Spectrum Bridge deployment is always in one of four explicit states, so degraded or unsafe conditions cannot stay implicit.

NOMINAL

All physics pre-gates passing. Signal quality within normative envelope. EBDP bounds not exceeded at any scale. Evidence Objects emitted with full provenance.

DEGRADED

One or more pre-gates marginal. EBDP bound approaching ceiling at one or more scales. Evidence Objects emitted with degradation flags. Human oversight recommended.

SAFE_MODE

Pre-gate failure or EBDP ceiling exceeded. Evidence emission suspended. Last-known-good snapshot committed. Downstream AI inference blocked until gate conditions restored.

HALT

Unrecoverable physics violation or security invariant breach. All emission halted. CAR written with HALT state. Manual intervention required for recovery.
