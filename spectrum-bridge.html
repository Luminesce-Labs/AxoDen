The four NASEM barriers — August 2025

Four RF-AI integration problems with no current solution

The National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine workshop on non-linguistic AI (August 2025) named four specific barriers at the RF-AI boundary. Spectrum Bridge is designed to address all four as formal assurance requirements, not engineering workarounds.

NASEM Barrier 1

No natural token boundaries

Language-centric AI safety approaches assume tokenisable input. Continuous RF waveforms have no natural token boundary. Standard EBDP stage gates cannot be directly applied without a tokenisation layer that preserves information content and physical meaning.

Spectrum Bridge S1: deterministic adaptive scale activation with token budgets derived from physical waveform characteristics, not arbitrary tokenisation.

NASEM Barrier 2

Throughput mismatch

RF sample rates (MHz to GHz) are orders of magnitude higher than AI inference rates. No existing framework manages the evidence-integrity implications of mandatory downsampling at this scale. Evidence admitted after downsampling may not be representative of the original signal.

Spectrum Bridge S2: per-scale quantisation preserving cross-platform determinism with EBDP bounds applied at each decimation stage.

NASEM Barrier 3

Shannon-limit ambiguity

RF signals near the Shannon capacity limit are physically ambiguous — multiple signal hypotheses are equally consistent with the observed waveform. Standard AI classification produces confident outputs on ambiguous inputs. Confidence is not a valid substitute for physical resolubility.

Spectrum Bridge S3: physics-informed pre-gates reject physically impossible or ambiguous signals before AI inference. MQ manages Shannon-limit states explicitly.

NASEM Barrier 4

Common-mode failure risk

Redundant AI systems processing the same RF signals from the same antenna array share common-mode failure risk. The CMRI independence assumption fails if redundant components share physical signal paths. Standard multi-agent independence assumptions do not account for shared RF infrastructure.

Spectrum Bridge: mathematical root independence on shared hardware enforced by CFS coprime monitor architecture. Shared RF path acknowledged as a constraint, not ignored.

Five-stage execution path

From RF signal to certified Evidence Object

The Spectrum Bridge execution path transforms raw RF signals into Evidence Objects that carry full cryptographic provenance and admissibility certification. Every stage is deterministic. The same input produces the same Evidence Object on every platform.

S1

Active development — 90-day target

Scale Activation — Deterministic Token Budgets

Deterministic adaptive scale activation derives token budgets from physical waveform characteristics. Scale selection is governed by entropy bounds, not heuristic thresholds. Token budget is committed into the Evidence Object provenance chain.

S2

Active development — 90-day target

Quantisation — Cross-Platform Determinism

Per-scale quantisation policy preserving cross-platform determinism. Bit-exact output guaranteed across hardware platforms within the quantisation policy envelope. EBDP bounds applied at each decimation stage prevent silent information loss.

S3

Active development — 90-day target

Physics Pre-Gates — Reject Before Inference

Physics-informed pre-gates enforce normative constraints derived from signal physics before any AI inference occurs. Physically impossible signals (below noise floor, outside bandwidth, Shannon-limit ambiguous) are rejected and logged. No AI model ever sees physically invalid input.

S4

Parallel design

Admissibility Gating — Evidence Quality Gate

G1-G4 gate sequence enforcing the normative operating envelope. Each gate evaluates a specific physical or informational constraint. A signal failing any gate is rejected, not downgraded. The gate outcomes are committed to the Evidence Object chain.

S5

Parallel design

Evidence Node Emission — CAR-Compatible EO

Evidence Objects with full cryptographic provenance: signal hash, scale and quantisation policy digest, gate outcomes, EBDP stage receipts, Ed25519 producer signature, RFC 3161 timestamp. Compatible with the CAR audit chain. Any independent party can verify the EO without access to the RF hardware.

Operating envelope

Four-state MQ operating envelope enforced at the RF boundary

The Monitoring Quad applies to the RF-AI boundary as directly as to any other domain. A Spectrum Bridge deployment is always in exactly one of four states. Transitions are deterministic, logged, and auditable.

NOMINAL

All physics pre-gates passing. Signal quality within normative envelope. EBDP bounds not exceeded at any scale. Evidence Objects emitted with full provenance.

DEGRADED

One or more pre-gates marginal. EBDP bound approaching ceiling at one or more scales. Evidence Objects emitted with degradation flags. Human oversight recommended.

SAFE_MODE

Pre-gate failure or EBDP ceiling exceeded. Evidence emission suspended. Last-known-good snapshot committed. Downstream AI inference blocked until gate conditions restored.

HALT

Unrecoverable physics violation or security invariant breach. All emission halted. CAR written with HALT state. Manual intervention required for recovery.
